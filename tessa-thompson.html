Tessa Thompson Biography

Tessa Lynne Thompson, professionally known as Tessa Thompson, was born on October 3, 1983. She is a young, multitalented American actress best known for playing Jackie Cook on Veronica Mars, Bianca in the film Creed, Valkyrie in Thor: Ragnarok, and Agent M in Men in Black: International.

She has also appeared in Dear White People, For Colored Girls, and Westworld in important roles.

Tessa attended Santa Monica High School before continuing her studies at Santa Monica College, where she obtained a degree in cultural anthropology.

Career

Tessa made her theatrical debut in 2002 as Ariel in a professional production of The Tempest by William Shakespeare. The next Shakespeare production in which she appeared was Romeo and Juliet: Antebellum New Orleans.

Tessa finally made her television debut in the CBS crime thriller Cold Case, which was made possible by the notoriety she gained through her theater performances. In the television drama, Tessa portrayed a 1930s gay smuggler, a character that has brought her considerable recognition over the years.

Afterward, she portrayed Jackie Cook in the noir drama series Veronica Mars. During the second season of the program, she became a regular cast member.

Her first cinematic role was in the horror flick When a Stranger Calls. Tessa plays the part of Scarlett in the film. This film enhanced her image as a graceful actress capable of portraying difficult characters.

As time passed, Tessa appeared in a few more films and television series, which expanded her Hollywood notoriety. A few years later, she was chosen to portray Bianca Taylor in the Rocky sequel film Creed. It was one of her most notable roles to date, and she repeated it in Creed II.

Tessa had an appearance as Valkyrie in Thor: Ragnarok at the same time. In the film, the crowd adored her, and her character became a fan favorite. She was requested to reprise her role in Avengers: Endgame and Thor: Love and Thunder, in which she would become the MCU’s first significant LGBT superhero.
