Christopher Lutz Biography

Christopher Lutz is one of the most popular and richest German chess grandmaster who was born on February 24, 1971 in Neukirchen-Vluyn, North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany. In 1971, Lutz achieved his title of international master in 1989. He also earned the title of grandmaster in 1992. He won the German Chess Championship in 1995 and 2001. In 2000, he played on board 4 in the German team which took home Silver in the 34th Chess Olympiad in Istanbul.

Lutz is the No. 28 player in the rankings of German player at the time of writing this article in May, with the FIDE score of 2537.

In the early months of 2006. Lutz worked as consultant to the Hydra project in chess. He focused on the creation of books for opening for Hydra and also developing test positions, up to the project was ended in 2009.
